# CS330

How do I approach designing software?
- I approach software design by breaking a large problem into smaller, more manageable components and organizing them into separate parts of the program. In my project, I structured the application by using distinct systems such as scene management, camera control, and rendering. This made the overall design easier to understand and also expand.

What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
- This project helped me to improve my ability to design modular and also reusable code. I learned how to separate responsibilites between classes such as utilizing SceneManager and ViewManager. It also strengthened my understanding of how different systems in a program interact.

What design process did you follow for your project work?
- My design process started with planning the overall 3D scene and coming up with a plan for the objects and features required. I then built the project incrementally, starting with basic shapes and gradually adding textures, lighting, and camera controls. This approach allowed me to refine each part before moving forward to the next one.

How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
- The modular design approach that was used here can be applied to future projects by keeping code organized and easier to mantain. Building systems in stages also helps reduce errors and makes debugging more efficient. This approach can also be used in larger software projects where complexity increases.

How do I approach developing programs?
- I approach program development by writing and testing small pieces of code before combingin them into the complete system. This helps me to ensure that each part is working correctly on its own. I also focus on readibility and structure so the program is easier to maintain.

What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
- While working on the 3D scene, I strategized by using incremental development and continuous testing. I frequently readjusted transformations, lighting, and camera movement to achieve the desired visual result. I also learned how to debug rendering issues by isolating specific parts of the scene.

How did iteration factor into your development?
- Iteration played a major role in refining both the functionality and visuals for me. I repeatedly tested changes to camera movement, object placement, and lighting until everything behaved correctly. Each iteration helped improve stability and visual quality.

How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?
- At the beginning, my focus was mainly on getting everything to work, even if it wasn't the optimal structure. Over time, after getting everything going correctly, I shifted toward cleaner organization and focused on adding design choices to the code. By the final milestone, my code was much more structured and easier to manage.
